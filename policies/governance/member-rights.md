## Purpose

This policy formalizes the rights and entitlements of each membership tier within Smoking Tigers Media (STM). It derives directly from the role definitions established in `role-authority.md` and is intended to make implicit entitlements explicit without creating new governance.

---

## Core Principle

Membership tier determines access, entitlements, and governance rights. **Rights are tied to role, not seniority or tenure.** All members operate under the parent constraints of Q1 Governance and the STM Charter.

---

## Membership Tiers

### Tier 1 — Executive Council (Stewards)

**Members:** Ed Hwang, Basil Childers, Van Nguyen, Christine Francis

Stewards are governance and capital allocators. They maintain Q1 alignment, oversee project intake, and hold final authority within their scope of responsibility.

#### Governance Access
- Full read/write access to the STM Governance Repository
- Authorized to propose, review, and approve governance changes per the contribution process
- May initiate decision records; hold authority to approve Class C governance actions
- Participate in all Executive Council decisions

#### Agent Entitlements
- Each Steward is entitled to a personal EA (Executive Assistant) agent
- EA agent operates under the individual Steward's scope; see `steward-ea-entitlement.md` for full policy
- EA agents carry the same capability scope as the primary EA (Eva): Notion API access, calendar, reminders, intake processing, daily summaries
- EA agents may spawn specialized sub-agents (content production, research, transcription) within the Steward's scope

#### Hub Access
- **Artist/Creator Hub:** Full access
- **Producer Hub:** Full access
- **Business Hub:** Full access

#### Voting and Decision Rights
- Full voting rights on governance decisions
- Authority to approve or veto proposals affecting their scope
- Authority to escalate to or escalate from Q1 Governance as appropriate
- No single Steward may unilaterally override collective Steward decisions on shared governance matters

#### Information Access
- Full access to all governance documents, decision records, and policy archives
- Full access to project-level records within their assigned projects
- Access to cross-project summaries and status reports
- Financial and RevPoint allocation records within their stewardship scope

---

### Tier 2 — Contributors

**Includes:** Project Contributors, Internal Builders, Executive Contributors (Council Members acting in contributor roles)

Contributors perform scoped labor and execution or governance/strategic labor. Their rights are scoped to the projects and systems they are actively engaged with.

#### Governance Access
- Read access to governance documents relevant to their project scope
- May propose governance improvements via the contribution process
- No direct write access to canonical governance records
- May not approve governance changes

#### Agent Entitlements
- Access to shared project agents and tools as provisioned per project
- No entitlement to a personal EA agent (Steward tier only)
- May be granted scoped tool access by a Steward for project-specific needs

#### Hub Access
- **Artist/Creator Hub:** Access granted per project role (Creator/Partner roles have access; Project Contributors access what is scoped to their project)
- **Producer Hub:** Access granted per project assignment
- **Business Hub:** Contributors receive **read-only access to business summaries relevant to their project only**. Full Business Hub access (write access or cross-project visibility) requires explicit Steward grant. This is a role-based access control — write access is restricted to qualified, responsible parties to prevent interference with financial and distribution records. See `hub-definitions.md` for full Business Hub access policy.

#### Voting and Decision Rights
- No vote on governance decisions
- May submit recommendations and proposals for Steward consideration
- Executive Contributors (acting in governance labor capacity) may participate in deliberations but do not hold approval authority

#### Information Access
- Access to project-level documents within their assigned scope
- Access to governance policies relevant to their role
- No access to cross-project financials or RevPoint allocation records outside their own compensation
- No access to other contributors' compensation or RevPoint records

---

### Tier 3 — Supporters

**Description:** Individuals providing strategic leverage — access, distribution, relationships — without labor obligations or governance participation.

#### Governance Access
- No governance repository access
- Not participants in governance deliberations or decisions

#### Agent Entitlements
- None

#### Hub Access
- Access granted on a case-by-case basis by a Steward
- Default: no hub access

#### Voting and Decision Rights
- No voting rights
- No governance participation rights
- May provide input when solicited by a Steward; input carries no formal weight

#### Information Access
- Limited to what is explicitly shared by a Steward for relationship or partnership purposes
- No access to internal governance, financial records, or contributor agreements

---

## Role Multiplicity

Per `role-authority.md`, individuals may simultaneously hold roles across tiers (e.g., a Steward who is also a Creator on a specific project). In cases of role multiplicity:

- Rights apply per role per context
- The higher-tier rights govern governance participation
- Project-specific rights apply only within declared project scope
- Conflicts of interest must be declared per project

---

## Entitlement Changes

- Rights changes (upgrades or downgrades) require Steward approval
- Entitlements tied to active service; inactive Contributors may have project-scoped access suspended
- Supporters may be granted temporary elevated access by Steward decision; reverts on project conclusion unless renewed

---

## Review Cadence

Review annually or upon:
- Any change to the membership tier structure
- Addition of new tiers or sub-roles
- Governance restructuring
