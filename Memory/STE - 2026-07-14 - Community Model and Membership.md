# Community Model and Membership

## Summary
Ed and Christine worked through the structure and value proposition of the Smoking Tigers community model. They agreed that the 6-week onboarding program framing (borrowed from the Solaris video) doesn't fit STE's approach — STE's model is modular and discovery-led: clients get only what they need, not a fixed package. The community's value is evergreen participation (staying current with AI as it evolves), not one-time consulting. The membership ask should initially be a content contribution (make a short video, share an idea) rather than a fee — with a paid modular service layer (e.g., employee upskilling at $500/person) as a separate revenue stream.

## Decisions
- Drop the 6-week program framing — use modular, discovery-led onboarding instead ("we figure out A, B, C vs. A, D based on your needs").
- Community value prop is evergreen participation — "stop trying to keep up alone and stop bleeding money on one-off consultants."
- Initial membership ask: contribute a short video or idea, not a membership fee.
- Paid revenue stream: employee upskilling/training as an add-on (e.g., $500/employee), separate from community membership.
- Community is positioned as a "trust network," not a solo-owned Build Club — cooperative framing is the differentiator.
- Business owners won't scroll/browse socially — community engagement must be efficient, not social-media-style.
