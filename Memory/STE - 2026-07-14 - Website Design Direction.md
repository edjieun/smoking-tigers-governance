# Website Design Direction

## Summary
Christine showed Ed a redesigned version of the website pages using a light-and-bright aesthetic (warm peach/gradient tones) as an alternative to the previous dark mode. Ed responded positively to the brightness and readability but pushed back gently on the pink tones for audience perception reasons. The agreed direction is bright, elegant, and gradient-warm — not dark, not pink-dominant. The critical structural decision was that the website's main hero section should center a ~60-second video (like the Solaris reference), with written copy sections available to scroll for those who want depth. Ed explicitly stepped back from design decisions to focus on copy.

## Decisions
- Visual direction: light/bright, warm gradient palette — not dark mode, not pink-dominant.
- One hard design rule from Ed: no clown themes. Ever.
- Website hero section: lead with a ~60-second video, written copy in supporting sections below.
- Ed owns copy; Christine owns design iteration.
- Video length target for hero: ~60 seconds (ad-length, suitable for the hero section and as a standalone paid ad).
