# AI Ops Consortium and Revenue Model

## Summary
Christine proposed building a consortium of 5–8 AI Ops companies (PandaBlocks + others she has been researching) as a unified Smoking Tigers partnership network — Smoking Tigers funnels leads to each company and takes a referral commission. Two offers emerge from this: (1) work-with-these-companies for clients (affiliate leads + media), and (2) a "set up your own consultancy" offer for individuals joining the ARC community. The Atomic Jobs Board is the eventual mechanism for the AI Ops community but explicitly NOT what gets pitched upfront. For media/YouTube: STE takes 80–90% of ad revenue from any content co-posted on the STE channel; partners get branding and links. RMA pivot agreed: RMA becomes a distribution channel for existing regenerative media content (Bloom Network, Danny's group) rather than the primary content producer. Ed wants content artifacts from every event — "events that leave no trace" are not acceptable.

## Decisions
- AI Ops consortium target: 5–8 companies; Christine to research and compile the list.
- Smoking Tigers YouTube ad revenue split: STE takes 80–90%; contributing partners get branding/links.
- RMA pivot: distribution channel for existing content, not primary producer.
- Atomic Jobs Board: concept confirmed as the long-term mechanism; NOT the pitch in partner outreach.
- Every event must produce content artifacts (recordings, written summaries, media).
- Event at The Gathering: potential "recording booth" or studio stage at Mubuhay Gardens for 20–30 conversations across the week.
