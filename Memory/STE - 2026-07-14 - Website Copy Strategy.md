# Website Copy Strategy

## Summary
Ed and Christine reviewed a reference video from Solaris as the messaging blueprint for the Smoking Tigers website. The video's structure — problem → solution → community CTA — was identified as the right roadmap for STE's own one-minute hero video and page copy. The core problem statement is that SMB owners and teams have adopted AI tools but remain stuck using them like chatbots, unable to bridge the last 10–20% gap that requires trusted human expertise. STE's differentiated angle is "human-first AI ops": not just tools, but a cooperative trust network of real practitioners who solve that last mile together.

## Decisions
- Adopt the Solaris video structure as the copy roadmap: problem → solution → community CTA.
- Target audience is established SMBs (with staff or solo operators), not enterprise — language should address "you've done everything right, now what?"
- Core problem statement: "Getting over the last 10% of AI isn't an AI problem — it's a human problem. You need trusted humans."
- Community ownership (cooperative model) is a differentiator but should NOT lead the front message; it belongs in secondary/supporting copy.
- Pursue two separate one-minute videos: one targeting clients/SMBs, one targeting AIOps practitioners.
