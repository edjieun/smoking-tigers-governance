# Content Strategy and Brand Positioning

## Summary
Ed proposed a shared YouTube channel among RMA, ARC, and SolarPunks to pool content toward YouTube Partnership Program requirements (1K subscribers + 4,000 watch hours for ad revenue). Christine pushed back on framing this as a VC pitch and reframed it as a regenerative economics experiment. Three audience segments were identified: vision/investor-level communities (SolarPunks, ARC, RMA), content creators ready to level up (500K–1M subscriber range), and general community/consultants at any stage. The "solar punk" label was rejected for Smoking Tigers' public brand — agreed positioning is "future of work" framing with solar punk ethos but not aesthetic. Concern raised that GRC (David Witzel's group) may already be moving ahead with their own content distribution deal via Lee Royale ("The Source").

## Decisions
- Smoking Tigers brand: adopt solar punk ethos, use **"future of work"** framing publicly — not "solar punk" label.
- STE is the intelligent connector/facilitator, not the builder of these communities.
- YouTube channel concept is valid as a regenerative economics project; not the investor pitch at events.
- Three audience segments to address in separate messaging: communities/investors, content creators, consultants.
- Distributed local discussion sessions (Brisbane, SF, Portugal) converging into a global Riverside call = a viable model for early programming.
