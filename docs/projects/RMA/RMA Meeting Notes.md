##### **B. Defining RMA’s Purpose & Scope**
**Core Debate: What Does RMA Do vs. What Does Smoking Tigers/Quorum1 Handle?**
- **Sage’s Proposal:**
  - RMA should focus on **collaborative, low-complexity support** (e.g., one-hour weekly meetings for project assistance).
  - Example: *"If I need three hours a week to help someone with their podcast, I’ll commit three hours myself. That’s the give-and-take we’re aiming for."*
  - Avoid duplication of MOUs/contracts; delegate those to Smoking Tigers.

- **Christine Francis’ Concerns:**
  - Hour-tracking complicates collective projects (e.g., Nikki’s documentary).
  - *"We’ve figured out that people get value from projects when they’re taken seriously, not just time-based."* — Christine
  - Proposed tracking contributions *asynchronously* (documenting effort without rigid hours).

- **Kurt Roskopf’s Perspective:**
  - Emphasized RMA’s role in **inspiring action** through media (e.g., Nikki’s project on food scarcity).
  - *"We need to figure out governance and protocols before we run into roadblocks."* — Kurt
  - Suggested outsourcing non-RMA functions (e.g., MOUs) to Smoking Tigers or Quorum1.

**Key Quote:**
> *"If RMA is just about being a connection mechanism, let’s keep it simple. If someone needs an MOU, they’ll go elsewhere."* — Sage Harper
