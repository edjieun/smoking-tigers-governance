**Relates to:** Notion, Google Calendar, Cal.com, Scheduling Agent

## Overview

Smoking Tigers uses a layered calendar architecture:
- **The Big Picture** — Notion calendar view (master view of all internal events/meetings)
- **Smoking Tigers — Operations** (Google Calendar) — shared internal calendar, synced to Google Meet and quorum.one
- **Smoking Tigers Calendar (Q1 Workspace)** — quorum.one Google Calendar (upgrade path, pending DWD grant)
- **Cal.com** — scheduling portal for booking meetings with the team

## 1. The Big Picture (Notion Calendar View)

**URL:** `https://www.notion.so/quorum1/2c56f6ac689e8079b9effd8e9ef39d59?v=2c56f6ac689e80c291ba000c80aed30b`

This is a calendar view of the Meetings database in Notion. It is the **primary internal view** of all team events.

### What goes here
- All internal meetings (exec, 1:1s, all-hands)
- Team events and milestones
- External meetings involving a team member
- Project kickoffs and reviews

### How events get here
When a meeting is added to the Notion Meetings DB, it automatically appears in The Big Picture (calendar view). The scheduling agent is responsible for creating/syncing these records.

## 2. Meeting Type → Platform Matrix

| Meeting Type | Platform | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| All Hands / Town Hall | Discord (Stage/Voice) | Internal-first; experimenting to replace Meet for internal |
| Exec / Leadership | Discord (Voice) | Internal video conferencing; replacing Huddles/Meet |
| 1:1 / Check-in | Discord (Voice) | Lightweight, persistent, international-friendly |
| Client / External | Zoom | Most universally accepted by external parties |
| Recorded / Production | Riverside | Best audio/video quality for produced content |
| Creative / Informal | Discord | Native home, no friction |
| Interviews / Partnerships | Zoom or Google Meet | Based on partner preference; default Zoom |
| Large Event / Webinar | Zoom | Scale and polling/Q&A features |

**Decision (2026-03-17):** Discord is the primary internal video conferencing platform (replacing Google Meet Huddles for internal meetings). External-facing defaults to Zoom.

## 3. Scheduling Agent Workflow

When a meeting is added to the Notion Meetings DB:

1. Agent reads: attendees (from Smoking Tigers Directory), meeting type, proposed time/duration
2. Looks up each attendee's quorum.one email (or primary email for non-Q1 members)
3. Checks Cal.com availability for internal attendees
4. Selects platform based on meeting type matrix (above)
5. Creates Google Calendar event on `Smoking Tigers — Operations` calendar
6. Sends invites via quorum.one calendar (Google Meet link auto-generated, or Zoom/Riverside/Discord link added)
7. Updates Notion Meetings DB record: status → Scheduled, adds calendar link, platform, meet link
8. Event appears in The Big Picture automatically

### Platform Link Generation
- **Google Meet:** Auto-generated by Google Calendar API
- **Zoom:** Via Zoom API (requires Zoom OAuth app — to be configured)
- **Riverside:** Manual link until API integration built; agent flags for human to add
- **Discord:** Agent posts channel/voice link from configured Discord server

## 4. SOP — User Account Setup

### Users WITH quorum.one accounts

**Step 1: Google Calendar**
- Your quorum.one account automatically has Google Workspace Calendar
- Subscribe to "Smoking Tigers — Operations" calendar (shared link — get from Ed or Scout)
- Set your working hours in Google Calendar settings (important for international scheduling)
- Enable "Show in The Big Picture" — this means adding events to the shared STM calendar, not personal

**Step 2: Cal.com**
- Go to `https://edlicious-server.tailebe6d3.ts.net:8443` (Tailscale required)
- Sign up with your quorum.one email
- Connect your Google Calendar so Cal.com sees your real availability
- Set your timezone and working hours
- Configure your event types (e.g., "30-min check-in", "1-hour project review")
- Share your Cal.com link in your Notion directory profile

**Step 3: Notion Directory**
- Ensure your quorum.one email is filled in your Smoking Tigers Team entry
- Add primary email if different
- Add preferred platform preference if you have one
- Add your timezone

**Step 4: Meeting Platforms**
- Google Meet: included with quorum.one — no extra setup
- Zoom: install Zoom, sign in with your preferred account (or quorum.one SSO if enabled)
- Riverside: account created per-project by production team — no standing account needed
- Discord: join the Smoking Tigers Discord server (invite from Ed or Scout)

---

### Users WITHOUT quorum.one accounts (External / Partners)

**Step 1: Calendar**
- You will receive Google Calendar invites to your primary email — accept and add to your calendar
- You do not need a quorum.one account to receive invites
- For recurring access, Ed or Scout will share specific calendar views with your email

**Step 2: Scheduling**
- Use the Cal.com booking link provided for your contact (get from Scout or your ST point of contact)
- Select meeting type when booking — platform will be auto-assigned

**Step 3: Notion**
- You may be given a guest access link to The Big Picture view
- You will not have edit access to the Meetings DB — your ST point of contact manages that

**Step 4: Meeting Platforms**
- Zoom: preferred for external meetings — join via link in your calendar invite
- Google Meet: also common — join via browser, no account needed
- Discord: optional — join the server if you're a regular collaborator
