## What a Q1 Creative Guild Is

A Q1 Creative Guild is a **purpose-driven contributor community** operating under Q1 governance:

- Defined mission and membership criteria
- Git-based decision tracking (transparent, auditable)
- Revenue Points as the contribution and ownership currency
- Multi-sided marketplace: contributors, projects, and capital all interact through a shared system

The guild model is designed for people who want to **build together with real economic alignment** — not just collaborate casually.

## Launch Playbook — Four Steps

### Step 1 — Form the Core (Now)
Recruit **3–4 founding members** who share a clear creative purpose.

This is not about audience — it's about **quality of the founding group**.

Define:
- What is this guild *for*? (e.g., "We build original IP in the gaming space — games, lore, tools, community")
- What kind of work counts as a contribution?
- Who is the founding steward?

Don't launch publicly until this core is aligned. The founding group sets the cultural and governance DNA.

---

### Step 2 — Launch a Private Governance Repo
Fork or mirror the Q1 governance model into a **private GitHub repo** for the guild.

This repo is where:
- Guild decisions are logged (DACI-style)
- Membership is tracked
- Projects are scoped and owned
- Changes to governance are proposed and approved

The repo is the **institutional memory** of the guild. It makes the community real — not just a chat.

Structure to start:
```
/decisions/       — decision log
/members/         — member directory + roles
/projects/        — active project profiles
/governance/      — rules, policies, contribution standards
README.md         — what this guild is, who it's for
```

---

### Step 3 — Start Building IP
Launch projects that contribute to the guild's mission.

In a gaming guild, this could include:
- Game concepts (design docs, lore, mechanics)
- Actual games (jam projects → full builds)
- Tools (engines, asset packs, dev utilities)
- Content (dev logs, streams, tutorials)
- Community infrastructure (governance templates, wikis)

Every project gets a **project profile** — scoped, owned, tracked.

Contributions are logged. Contributors earn Revenue Points for their work.

---

### Step 4 — Revenue Points as Contribution Tokens
Revenue Points (RP) are how the guild tracks and rewards contribution.

**How it works:**
- RP have a fixed USD face value
- Members purchase RP directly (buying in = staking in the collective mission)
- RP are earned through verified contributions (design, dev, writing, production)
- When a project generates revenue, RP holders participate in distribution

**The arcade analogy:**
> Instead of playing games to win tokens, guild members *build* games to earn RP. The tokens are real — backed by real value in real projects.

**Why this matters:**
- It's not equity (no securities complexity in early stage)
- It's not just a points game (it's redeemable against real revenue)
- It creates **economic alignment without bureaucracy**
- It makes contribution legible and fair
