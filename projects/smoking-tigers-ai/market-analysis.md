# **Smoking Tigers AI**

## **1\. Market Definition (What Market This Actually Competes In)**

Smoking Tigers AI does **not** sit cleanly in a single category. That’s both the risk *and* the advantage.

It operates at the intersection of four markets:

### **A. AI Productivity & Knowledge Assistants**

Examples: Notion AI, ChatGPT Enterprise

**Market size:** Very large, very noisy

**Primary buyer:** Knowledge workers, teams, enterprises

**Core promise:** Faster writing, summarization, answers

### **B. Meeting Intelligence & Decision Capture**

Examples: Fireflies, Otter, Fathom

**Market size:** Large and growing

**Primary buyer:** Sales, managers, remote teams

**Core promise:** Don’t forget what was said

### **C. Knowledge Management / Enterprise Search**

Examples: Glean, Guru

**Market size:** Large, enterprise-skewed

**Primary buyer:** Mid-market to enterprise ops teams

**Core promise:** Find institutional knowledge fast

### **D. Ops / Chief-of-Staff Augmentation (Underserved)**

Examples: *No clean category*

**Market size:** Smaller but high willingness to pay

**Primary buyer:** Founders, execs, creative leads

**Core promise:** Reduce coordination load and decision decay

👉 **Smoking Tigers AI competes most directly in D**, while borrowing functionality from A–C.

This is critical:

You are not selling “AI productivity.”

You are selling **operational clarity and governance-preserving coordination**.

## **3\. Competitive Landscape (Direct & Adjacent)**

### **Category 1: General AI Assistants**

#### **ChatGPT Enterprise**

**Strengths**

* Best-in-class models

* Familiar UX

* Easy to deploy

**Weaknesses (vs STM AI)**

* Cloud-first, not private-first

* No governance model

* No concept of decision promotion

* Stateless unless heavily integrated

* Encourages “AI as answer machine”

**Net:**

Powerful brain, no memory discipline, no authority awareness.

---

#### **Notion AI**

**Strengths**

* Embedded in work context

* Familiar to teams

* Good for drafting and summarizing

**Weaknesses**

* Notion becomes both thinking space *and* truth

* No clear draft vs governance boundary

* AI can quietly reshape canonical docs

* No concept of human-in-the-loop promotion

**Net:**

Great assistant, dangerous system-of-record ambiguity.

---

### **Category 2: Meeting Intelligence Tools**

#### **Fireflies.ai**

**Strengths**

* Excellent transcription

* Strong integrations

* Scales easily

**Weaknesses**

* Treats meetings as endpoints

* “Action items” are shallow

* No governance or authority model

* Decisions are inferred, not committed

**Net:**

Captures conversations, not outcomes.

---

#### **Otter.ai**

**Strengths**

* Accurate transcripts

* Easy adoption

**Weaknesses**

* Notes ≠ decisions

* No institutional memory

* No lifecycle from talk → truth

**Net:**

Archive tool, not an operating system.

---

### **Category 3: Knowledge Management & Search**

#### **Glean**

**Strengths**

* Powerful enterprise search

* Integrates many systems

* Great for scale

**Weaknesses**

* Enterprise-only pricing and complexity

* Assumes clean internal systems

* No concept of governance or authority

* Overkill for creator-led orgs

**Net:**

Search engine for big companies, not coordination for small ones.

---

### **Category 4: Workflow Automation**

#### **Zapier**

**Strengths**

* Massive ecosystem

* Easy automation

**Weaknesses**

* Automates before understanding

* No decision logic

* No human review loop

* Encourages silent state changes

**Net:**

Great plumbing, dangerous without governance.
