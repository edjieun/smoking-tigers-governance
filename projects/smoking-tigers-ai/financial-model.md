# Recovery Cap

You’re right — the **recovery cap has to be derived from the traditional (cash-cost) model first**, then we decide how much of that cash-cost burden we can safely **defer** into RevPoints.

Here’s the correct order and the cap math using the **traditional 10-year projection we already laid out**.

---

## **1\) Start with the traditional model totals (baseline capacity)**

From the earlier “traditional” projection:

* **10-year total revenue:** **$20.051M**

* **10-year total EBITDA (approx):** **$11.366M**

That EBITDA is your *projected operating surplus* before deciding how much can be allocated to RevPoint recovery.

---

## **2\) Define the recovery pool policy (what % of surplus can go to RP)**

A recovery cap isn’t just a number — it’s the **maximum cumulative amount you’re willing to allocate from future performance** to repay deferred early costs.

Two clean policy options:

### **Option A (simplest): Cap as % of cumulative EBITDA**

This is the most “business-real” because it ties repayment to actual operating capacity.

Example cap bands (based on $11.366M EBITDA):

* **Conservative cap \= 20% of EBITDA:** 0.20 × 11.366M \= **$2.27M**

* **Base cap \= 30% of EBITDA:** 0.30 × 11.366M \= **$3.41M**

* **Aggressive cap \= 40% of EBITDA:** 0.40 × 11.366M \= **$4.55M**

### **Option B (revenue-based): “Recovery pool \= X% of revenue”**

This is simpler to communicate but less safe (because revenue ≠ surplus).

If you used **20% of revenue** as the maximum lifetime recovery pool:

* 0.20 × 20.051M \= **$4.01M**

---

## **3\) Apply a “downside haircut” (because projections aren’t guarantees)**

If you want the cap to stay non-extractive even under underperformance, take a haircut.

A reasonable haircut is **30%** (i.e., assume you only realize 70% of the plan).

Using the **Base cap (30% of EBITDA \= $3.41M)**:

* Haircut 30% → $3.41M × 0.70 \= **$2.39M**

So the *risk-adjusted* base cap is about **$2.4M**.

---

## **4\) Recommendation (so we can proceed)**

If you want a cap that is:

* defensible

* non-extractive

* still meaningful enough to defer real early cost

**Recommended Recovery Cap (Base, risk-adjusted): $2.4M**

**Recommended Recovery Cap (Base, no haircut): $3.4M**

Then the next step (now in the right order) is:

1. decide the cap (pick one), then

2. decide the **annual recovery rule** (e.g., “up to 20% of EBITDA each year until cap is met”), then

3. decide how many RevPoints you can issue early without violating that cap.

If you tell me whether you want the **risk-adjusted cap ($2.4M)** or the **plan cap ($3.4M)**, I’ll rebuild the RevPoints version cleanly with:

* allowed annual recovery pool,

To avoid ambiguity, we’re using **your established meanings**:

* **RevPoints (RP):**

   A trust-based participation credit representing *deferred cost or risk absorbed*.

* **RPM (Revenue Participation Multiplier):**

   The **maximum multiple** of the original RevPoint value that can be recovered from future revenue.

RPM answers the question:  
**“What is the maximum upside a RevPoint contributor can receive?”**

\\textbf{RPM}\_{\\text{max}} \= \\frac{\\text{Total Recovery Cap}}{\\text{Total RevPoints Issued}}
